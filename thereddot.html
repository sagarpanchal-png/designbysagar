Overview

TheRedDot is an inclusive and gender-sensitive menstrual health and hygiene (MHH) platform designed for Asha workers, adolescents, parents, teachers, NGOs, and subject matter experts. The platform respects user pronouns and acknowledges experiences across the gender spectrum.

Role

UI/UX Designer & UX Researcher

Duration

12 weeks (October 2021 - December 2022)

Tools

Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Xd, Lottie files

The Problem

The challenge was to design a multilingual and inclusive platform that promotes menstrual health awareness, provides a medium for Asha workers to spread awareness, facilitates interactive content sharing in a safe and respectful environment, and guarantees user privacy.

Solution

The final solution is an inclusive, multilingual MHH platform offering interactive and respectful content sharing, user engagement features, and a chat feature. Additionally, it has offline capabilities, allowing content to be downloaded for later consumption. The platform has facilitated increased awareness of menstrual health and hygiene among its diverse user base, stressing the importance of interactivity and inclusivity in digital platforms.

Reflections and Learnings

  • Designing TheRedDot platform highlighted the critical role of language and inclusivity in design when targeting a diverse user base.
  • It underscored the importance of interactivity in digital platforms to improve user engagement and the need for robust content moderation.
  • The challenges and rewards of designing for a sensitive and essential topic like menstrual health and hygiene were also experienced.
